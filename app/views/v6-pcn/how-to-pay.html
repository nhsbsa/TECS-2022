You didn't have a valid medical exemption certificate at the time, but you can still apply

Although you didn't have a valid medical exemption certificate when you claimed a free prescription, you still have the chance to apply.

Pay the prescription charge now

You will need to pay the original £35.20 prescription charge.

When asked to enter the amount you are paying, you just need to type £17.60 (not the full Penalty Charge Notice amount)

Pay now

Apply for a medical exemption certificate

Information:

You only have until the 07 May 2023 to get a medical exemption certificate.

Ask your doctor how to apply.

You can only apply for a medical exemption certificate if your condition is on the accepted list (below). If you're unsure about the name of your condition or treatment, check with your doctor.

List of accepted medical conditions
  • cancer and are undergoing treatment
    including treatment for the effects of cancer or the effects of cancer treatment.
  • diabetes (type 1 or type 2)
    if you need medication to keep blood sugar levels stable.
  • diabetes insipidus
    and other forms of hypopituitarism.
  • a continuing physical disability
    which means you can't go out without the help of another person.
  • epilepsy
    if you need continuous anticonvulsive therapy
  • a permanent fistula
    which needs continuous surgical dressing or an appliance - for example, caecostomy, colostomy, laryeigostom or ileostomy.
  • hypoadrenalism
    where specific substitution therapy is essential - for example, Addison's Disease.
  • hypoparathyroidism
    which means your parathyroid glands produce too little parathyroid hormone.
  • myasthenia gravis
    where ana autoimmune condition affects your nerves and muscles.
  • myxoedema
    for example, hypothyroidism which needs thyroid hormone replacement.

What happens next?

After your medical exemption certificate application has been submitted, an automatic check is carried out. You do not need to telephone us.

If your application has been successful and you have paid the £17.60 your Penalty Charge Notice will be closed. You don't need to do anything more.

Information:

If your doctor tells you that you are not eligible for a medical exemption certificate you will need to pay the full amount of the Penalty Charge Notice.
